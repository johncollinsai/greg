AI analyst for due diligence

Welcome to my personal space where I build and scale AI using Deep Learning, Language Models, and GPTs. Here, I share my work and explore AI topics that I find interesting.

This site is a composite of several AI applications and a variety of HTML-based posts. Each of my AI applications is architected as a microservice, functioning independently while also forming part of an integrated ecosystem.

The front end uses Flask, a lightweight WSGI web application framework, along with the combination of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to provide intuitive and responsive UI. Flask's ease of use and flexibility is ideal for my purposes.

My platform is hosted on Google Cloud, utilizing Nginx as a web server and proxy, and orchestrated through Kubernetes (K8s) for managing the microservices, ensuring smooth deployment, scaling, and networking.

The source code that drives my platform resides across multiple repositories on my GitHub. This multi-repo strategy facilitates modular development and efficient version control. If you're interested in delving deeper into the mechanics behind the services you see here, you're welcome to explore the GitHub repositories to understand my codebase further.

How to use your AI analyst for due diligence

Have you ever wondered why the LLM you are using, say ChatGPT or Claude, provides a lot of additional information that you are not necessarily looking for and why certain other apps that use the same underlying LLM/GPT models provide more targeted responses? One way we obtain more specific answers from our models is through "prompt engineering".

In this post, I talk about prompt engineering, share insights from my experience, and describe some commonly used approaches.

Prompt engineering involves a deep understanding of the model's capabilities, idiosyncrasies, and limitations, making it a blend of both science and art. Implementing apps that use prompt engineering can be technically challenging, so it requires coding and deployment skills too.
